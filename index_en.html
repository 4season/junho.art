Undergraduate Researcher

Hello, I'm Junho Heo, an Aspiring Researcher.

I am an undergraduate researcher majoring in Computer Engineering at Baekseok University, aspiring to bridge machine learning and cognitive science to unlock the potential of Computational Psychiatry.
Driven by constant questioning throughout my learning and exploration journey, I document my path of growth.

Turning complexity into clarity is an art.

Facing cognitive and psychiatric challenges such as ADHD, narcolepsy, and dyslexia, I experienced firsthand how learning efficiency varies based on attention and arousal. What began as a personal struggle eventually transformed into a scientific inquiry: Can individual differences in learning and decision-making be explained through computational and cognitive processes?

As I explored machine learning modeling in my computer engineering studies, I realized the necessity of computational models to explain human behavior and learning, moving beyond the interpretability limits of artificial neural networks. My goal is to become a researcher in Computational Psychiatry, using computational neuroscience models to study behavioral and neural data to understand the mechanisms of cognitive difficulties and develop personalized interventions.
