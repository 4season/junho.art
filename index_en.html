Undergraduate Researcher

Hello, I'm Junho Heo, an aspiring researcher.

I am an undergraduate researcher majoring in Computer Science at Baekseok University, studying human learning and decision-making from the perspectives of Computational Neuroscience and Computational Psychiatry.

Finding structure in complex problems is an art.

ADHD and narcolepsy have sometimes made learning and daily life difficult, but they also became important starting points for my interest in differences in human attention, arousal, and learning.

I began with the observation that even the same person can process information and make judgments differently depending on their attentional and arousal state. From there, I became interested in individual differences in cognition and behavior.

Currently, I am building a foundation in data preprocessing, machine learning model implementation, and behavioral and neural data analysis through computer science. In the long term, I hope to use computational models alongside behavioral and neural data to explain human learning, decision-making, and cognitive individual differences.

My Journey

A timeline of how I am building research capabilities in Computational Neuroscience and Computational Psychiatry through a foundation in Computer Science.

Education | Spring 2021 ~ Education

Enrolled at Baekseok University and Received National Scholarship

Began building core skills in programming, algorithms, and system design through foundational computer science coursework. In my first semester, I received the National Scholarship (Type II, Spring 2021), which helped provide a stable starting point for my studies.

Award | Fall 2023 Award

UML Object-Oriented Modeling Competition Excellence Award

Awarded the Excellence Award for designing system component interactions and data flows. This experience helped me learn how to structure complex problems and express them as logical models, which later shaped my interest in computational modeling.

Honor | Spring 24, Fall 25 & Spring 26 Scholarship

Received Professor Recommendation Scholarship Three Times

Awarded the Professor Recommendation Scholarship three times (Spring 2024, Fall 2025, & Spring 2026) in recognition of my academic diligence and research potential. The recognition encouraged me to develop my research direction with greater focus.

Activity | August 2025 Activity

Completed DIP SW Convergence Talent Program

Completed the Software Convergence Talent Development Program organized by the Daegu Digital Innovation Promotion Agency (DIP), strengthening my software development skills and gaining experience collaborating on team projects.

Projects | Fall 2025 ~ Present Projects

Project-based Development & AI Data Modeling

Following the DIP program, I focused on AI development and data modeling. I built the Magnolia chatbot, an MLP model predicting Seongnam City Bus Route 109 delays, and a CNN-based Drug-Drug Interaction (DDI) prediction model.

Research | Summer 2026 ~ Present Researcher

Brain-AI Interface (BAI) lab Undergraduate Researcher

I joined the Brain-AI Interface (BAI) Lab as an undergraduate researcher, where I am setting up the initial research environment and learning preprocessing workflows and analysis pipelines for neural data. Through hands-on research experience, I am building a foundation that connects to Computational Neuroscience and Computational Psychiatry.

Future Roadmap | Graduate Studies Research Plan

Computational Modeling of Neural Data and Cognitive Science

I aim to pursue graduate studies to study reinforcement learning, probabilistic modeling, and computational neuroscience in depth.
