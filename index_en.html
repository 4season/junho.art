Undergraduate Researcher

Hello, I'm Junho Heo, an aspiring researcher.

I am a computer science undergraduate researcher at Baekseok University, exploring human learning and decision-making through Computational Neuroscience and Computational Psychiatry.

Turning complexity into clarity is an art.

Having lived with cognitive and psychiatric challenges such as ADHD, narcolepsy, and dyslexia, I experienced firsthand how learning efficiency and decision-making can vary with attention and arousal states. What began as a personal struggle gradually became a scientific question: can these differences be explained as individual variation in computational and cognitive processes?

Through computer science, machine learning, and neural network modeling, I came to see the need for computational models that connect data with human behavior and learning. My goal is to become a researcher in Computational Psychiatry, using computational neuroscience methods to analyze behavioral and neural data, clarify the mechanisms behind cognitive difficulties, and support personalized interventions.

Following the DIP program, I focused on AI development and data modeling. I built the Magnolia chatbot, an MLP model predicting Seongnam City Bus Route 109 delays, and a CNN-based Drug-Drug Interaction (DDI) prediction model.

Participated as an undergraduate researcher at the Brain-AI Interface (BAI) Lab, setting up the initial research environment and learning preprocessing workflows and analysis pipelines for neural data. Through hands-on laboratory experience, I am building the research foundation for Computational Neuroscience and Computational Psychiatry.
